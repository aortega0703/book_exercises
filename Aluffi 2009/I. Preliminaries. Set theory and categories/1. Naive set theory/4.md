How many different equivalence relations may be defined on the set $\{1, 2, 3\}$?
##### Solution:
There are $5$ equivalence corresponding to the partitions
- $\{\{1\},\{2\},\{3\}\}$
- $\{\{1, 2\},\{3\}\}$
- $\{\{1, 3\},\{2\}\}$
- $\{\{1\},\{2, 3\}\}$
- $\{\{1, 2, 3\}\}$
