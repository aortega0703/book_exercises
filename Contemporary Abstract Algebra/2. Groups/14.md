Show that the group $\operatorname{GL}(2, \mathbb{R})$ of Example $9$ is non-Abelian by exhibiting a pair of matrices $A$ and $B$ in $\operatorname{GL}(2, \mathbb{R})$ such that $AB \neq BA$.
##### Solution:
$$
\begin{bmatrix}
	0 & 1\\
	1 & 0
\end{bmatrix}
\begin{bmatrix}
	0 & 1\\
	0 & 1
\end{bmatrix}
=
\begin{bmatrix}
	0 & 1\\
	0 & 1
\end{bmatrix}
\neq
\begin{bmatrix}
	1 & 0\\
	1 & 0
\end{bmatrix}
=
\begin{bmatrix}
	0 & 1\\
	0 & 1
\end{bmatrix}
\begin{bmatrix}
	0 & 1\\
	1 & 0
\end{bmatrix}
$$
